B2C Launch · Kiosk Design · Sustainability

JuicOrganic

Vending machine UI for an organic juice brand

JuicOrganic had strong products and a clear ecological profile, but no way to reach consumers directly. The brief was open: get them in front of everyday people. We chose the vending machine direction, then had to solve the real challenge. Design a kiosk that works in under five minutes, in a busy transit location, for someone who's never used it before.

The Challenge

JuicOrganic was a B2B juice brand ready to go direct-to-consumer. Once we identified commuters and on-the-go buyers as the primary target group, the UX problem became clear. The kiosk had to work in a noisy transit environment, in under five minutes, for a first-time user with no patience for friction. Every extra step was a lost sale.

What We Did

  • Researched vending machine design globally and observed real users at Copenhagen Central Station to understand buying behaviour
  • Interviewed two target users (a commuter and a project manager) to understand what matters to them
  • Tested design ideas with real users, including the logo, layout options, and the full purchase flow
  • Built a working prototype with a touchscreen interface, inventory management system, and bottle reward program
  • Created a design system to keep everything consistent and easy to update
  • Produced a campaign video and social media strategy for launch

The Outcome

We delivered a working coded prototype, a complete Figma design system, and a full communication strategy. The biggest UX gains came from cutting steps, not adding features. After iteration, users could complete a purchase in under two minutes. The key insight: in a vending machine context, clarity always beats creativity.

EdTech · Inclusive Design · Character Design

GradeAid: The Deep Blue

Reimagining gamification for neurodivergent children

GradeAid had working AI technology for teaching neurodivergent children, but needed a visual identity, characters, and a way to make the experience feel safe. When we researched the target group, we found that standard EdTech design could actively overstimulate the very users it was meant to support. Traditional gamification creates a dopamine chase. Bright colours and busy interfaces trigger sensory overload. We had to rethink the whole thing from the ground up.

The Challenge

The client had a working AI teaching platform and needed a visual identity, a logo, and animated characters to guide children through the app. The target group was neurodivergent children and youth, primarily those with ADHD, autism, and sensory processing disorders. Many experienced school refusal. Our research revealed that existing EdTech choices assumed a neurotypical learner. High-contrast colours, competitive reward structures, and busy interfaces were creating the very overstimulation they were meant to help with.

What We Did

  • Researched how neurodivergent children learn and what design choices cause overstimulation
  • Interviewed a special needs teacher to validate our approach
  • Designed a calm, water-inspired visual system with soft colours and gentle animations
  • Created three ocean-creature characters that grow and change as children progress
  • Built two versions of the interface: one playful for younger kids, one darker and more mature for teenagers
  • Added a focus mode with calming sounds to help children regulate their attention
  • Tested the design with a real child and refined based on feedback

The Outcome

What began as a visual identity and character brief became a full product reframe. Every design decision (colour, motion, typography, reward structure) was grounded in neurological research rather than aesthetics. The child test confirmed the calmer direction worked. The expert validated the gamification logic. The key learning: for neurodivergent users, design has to earn its choices.

FinTech · Startup · Brand & UX

Econiq

Visual identity and product redesign for an AI forecasting tool

A fintech founder had a working AI concept but no visual identity and a prototype that was hard to navigate. We built the brand from scratch with colour, typography, and logo, then redesigned the whole product structure in close collaboration with him.

The Challenge

The concept was strong, but the prototype lacked structure. There was no navigation, inconsistent layouts, and an onboarding flow where people dropped off. We also needed to build a visual identity from scratch. The real challenge was making financial forecasting and dashboard data feel approachable for small business owners who weren't finance experts.

What We Did

  • Built the visual identity from scratch. We created a colour palette, typography system, and several logo directions, working closely with the founder to understand what he wanted his brand to feel like
  • Mapped out the user experience problems. The navigation was confusing, the steps weren't clear, and there was too much information on each screen
  • Redesigned the interface to walk users through the forecasting process one step at a time
  • Simplified the onboarding so new users could get started on their own
  • Talked to a finance expert who gave us feedback on the visual design and how we organized the information. This helped us make sure the interface made sense for financial data
  • Tested with real small business owners to see if it actually worked for people without a finance background

The Outcome

We delivered two things the founder didn't have when we started. A visual identity he could build on, and a product structure that actually made sense to use. In testing, users could complete the forecasting flow and read the dashboard without help. That was a real improvement from where we began.

HOW WE WORK

Research-first. Every time.

Every design decision is backed by user testing, anthropological thinking, and logic, not just aesthetics. We challenge each other's assumptions, iterate quickly, and don't skip steps just to be done.

Step 01 Understand

We talk to you, your users, and anyone close to the problem. Before we design anything, we need to know what's actually going on.

Step 02 Define

We map out the problem, the user journey, and what a good solution looks like. This is where the direction gets set.

Step 03 Design & Test

From rough sketches to high-fidelity screens. We test with real users and refine until it works.

Step 04 Build & Deliver

A coded, working prototype you can actually click through, plus documentation and a clean handover.
