SaaS · Vending Machine · Sustainability

JuicOrganic

Smart Vending Machine Kiosk UI

UX RESEARCH · INTERACTION DESIGN · FRONT-END DEVELOPMENT

How do you sell a product in a hurry, and still nudge more sustainable behaviour? For JuicOrganic's shift from B2B to direct-to-consumer, we designed a smart vending machine kiosk with a reward loop for reusing bottles — and built the whole thing in code.

The Challenge

An organic juice brand had a strong product but no direct consumer channel. The brief was open: get them in front of consumers. Once we chose the vending machine direction, the core UX problem became speed — the interface had to work in busy transit locations where people abandon anything that feels slow or confusing.

What We Did

  • User interviews and personas to understand on-the-go purchasing behaviour and attitudes toward sustainability
  • Journey maps and A/B testing to find the fastest path from product discovery to payment
  • Design system in Figma with variables — covering kiosk UI, bottle branding, and the reward loop
  • Coded front-end prototype (HTML, SCSS, JavaScript, Bootstrap) optimised for iPad Pro
  • Campaign video in After Effects and a B2C content strategy for launch

The Outcome

We delivered a working coded prototype, a high-fidelity Figma design system, and a promotional video. A fast kiosk experience comes from cutting steps and making decision points obvious — not adding features. The project received top marks (12).

EdTech · Neuro-Inclusive · Motion Design

GradeAid — The Deep Blue

Neuro-Inclusive Gamified Learning Concept

GAMIFICATION DESIGN · UX RESEARCH · MOTION DESIGN

A DTU-backed EdTech startup needed a visual identity for an AI learning app targeting neurodivergent children. When we researched the target group, we found that standard EdTech design could actively overstimulate the very users it was meant to support. We rethought the whole experience.

The Challenge

The client had working AI technology but needed a visual identity, a logo, and animated characters to guide children through the app. Our research revealed that existing EdTech choices — bright colour palettes, busy interfaces, high-stakes reward structures — assumed a neurotypical learner. We needed an experience grounded in how neurodivergent brains process visual information, motivation, and failure.

What We Did

  • Desk research on neuro-inclusive design, Blue Mind theory, and WCAG colour science
  • Expert interview with a special needs teacher on colour, motivation, and gamification design
  • Designed a 5-phase user journey (discovery → advocacy) to map needs and risks across the full experience
  • Built a gamification system: coral reef metaphor, XP/currency loop, reward shop, and age-adaptive progression
  • Co-designed a character system with tintable skins and age-adaptive evolution for long-term engagement
  • Produced character motion in After Effects with slow, fluid pacing to reduce overstimulation risk

The Outcome

What began as a visual identity brief became a full product reframe — calmer motion, research-backed colour decisions, and a structured gamification loop. We validated the direction through an expert interview and an early test session with one child. The project received top marks (12).

FinTech · UX Research · Information Architecture

Econiq

FinTech SaaS Prototype

UX LEAD · INFORMATION ARCHITECTURE · USER TESTING

A FinTech founder had a solid concept — an AI-powered forecasting tool for small business owners — but the prototype was hard to navigate. We led the UX redesign, rebuilt the information architecture, tightened the onboarding, and delivered a user-tested prototype that non-experts could complete without help.

The Challenge

The concept was strong, but the prototype (built with AI tools) lacked structure: no navigation, inconsistent layouts, and an onboarding flow with high drop-off risk. The core UX challenge was making financial forecasting and dashboard data approachable for small business owners without a finance background.

What We Did

  • Audited the original prototype and documented usability and IA issues
  • Rebuilt the information architecture and introduced a clear navigation model
  • Redesigned onboarding to reduce steps and increase clarity at each decision point
  • Built the redesigned flow in Figma
  • Validated with 5 think-aloud usability tests with members of a local startup community
  • Expert review with a financial instructor to validate domain-specific assumptions

The Outcome

We delivered a usable foundation for launch — a coherent product structure and a working prototype the founder accepted as the direction forward. In testing, users could complete the forecasting questionnaire and read the dashboard without assistance. A clear improvement over the original flow.

HOW WE WORK

Research-first. Every time.

Every design decision is backed by user testing, anthropological thinking, and logic — not just aesthetics. We challenge each other's assumptions, iterate quickly, and don't skip steps just to be done.

1. Understand

We talk to you, your users, and anyone close to the problem. Before we design anything, we need to know what's actually going on.

2. Define

We map out the problem, the user journey, and what a good solution looks like. This is where the direction gets set.

3. Design & Test

From rough sketches to high-fidelity screens. We test with real users and refine until it works.

4. Build & Deliver

A coded, working prototype you can actually click through — plus documentation and a clean handover.
