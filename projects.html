My Projects

JuicOrganic – “Say Yes to Less Plastic” | SaaS-solution

For our 2nd-semester exam project, we developed a SaaS-based concept for JuicOrganic’s transition from B2B to B2C. The solution centered on a smart vending machine that offers sustainable juice refills and promotes eco-friendly habits through digital interactivity.

I worked on UX research (interviews, personas, user journeys, and data analysis), information architecture, and front-end coding using JavaScript, HTML, SCSS, and Bootstrap. Additionally, I created the After Effects video used as the vending machine's promotional video and social media campaign element.

The result was a user-centered prototype combining sustainability, accessibility, and technology in a cohesive digital experience.

Hoedown After Dark - Queer Cowboy Apparel | E‑commerce Project

For this project, we developed Hoedown After Dark — a queer and kink‑inspired fashion brand celebrating self‑expression, club culture, and community. The concept merges Western aesthetics with techno and fetish influences to create a bold, subversive visual identity.

I managed the group project process using a Kanban board and worked on SEO, business modelling, persuasive design, Figma prototyping, and product development. I also contributed to SoMe content and visual identity design.

The result was a strong brand identity and tested product designs developed through the Lean Startup method, real‑world feedback, and close engagement with Copenhagen's queer community. All earnings from the project were donated to LGBT Asylum.

Das Büro - Digital Design Studio | Client Cases

In this currently ongoing project, our team of 8 is working as a digital design bureau, delivering visual identity, branding, Figma prototypes and more for real clients. The focus is on creating user‑centred, impactful designs that meet client needs while maintaining strong creative cohesion.

My roles

  • Leading project coordination for a distributed team, including task delegation, stand‑up meetings, and overall workflow management using Kanban and the Double Diamond design process.
  • Conducting UX research, client meetings, and managing client communication to ensure designs align with business goals.
  • Supporting design work through Figma prototypes and contributing to visual identity development.

Through this role, I am strengthening my project management skills, cross‑timezone team coordination, client communication, and UX‑focused design process.

Gestalt - Digital Magazine | Visual design

Theme: Echo Chambers

Gestalt is a collaborative digital magazine developed as part of the Visual Design for Digital Platforms course at Zealand. The 2025 issue explores the theme Echo Chambers and how digital media shape our perception of reality.

I contributed an article interpreting the theme through both text and visuals. My work included writing, designing layouts and posters, and taking all photographs. Throughout the project, I applied principles of grid systems, visual hierarchy, and typography to create a cohesive and engaging editorial narrative.
