# Vasuqi — Tone of Voice & Social Media Guide

## What the brand sounds like

Vasuqi is a deep-tech clean-water company at investor stage. The voice is calm, confident, and direct. It does not perform excitement. It does not hedge. It states what the problem is, and what light does about it.

The single principle that governs all copy: **"Stupidly simple."**
Simple is not a limitation. Simple is the argument.

---

## The five voice rules

### 1. Short claim, then evidence
Lead with the conclusion. Earn it after.

> **"No chemicals added."**  
> Vasuqi's photocatalytic process activates on visible light — the disinfection mechanism is physical, not chemical.

Never open with context and bury the point. The brand's signature rhythm is claim-first.

### 2. Light, not dark
Even when the topic is water pollution, regulatory failure, or industrial waste — the frame is forward-looking. What becomes possible, not what is broken.

> ✓ "Clean water, by light."  
> ✗ "The water crisis is getting worse."

Vasuqi is not a warning. It is a solution.

### 3. Anti-hype — subtract, don't add
Build trust by listing what is absent. Short, negative-space statements prove simplicity by what they leave out.

> "No sludge generated. No chemicals added. No moving parts."

Most brands add claims. Vasuqi removes complexity. That is the claim.

### 4. Never compare, only complete
Vasuqi does not attack competitors. It claims the gap they leave.

> ✓ "Finish what conventional treatment can't."  
> ✗ "Better than ozonation."  
> ✗ "Unlike traditional UV systems..."

Comparisons cede ground. Completion statements claim it.

### 5. The regulatory anchor
One argument is always available: the pressure is structural, not optional. Water quality targets are set by EU law. The market has to move. Vasuqi is where it moves to.

> "The targets are set. The timeline is fixed. The solution is light."

This line, or its equivalent, belongs in most posts.

## What to post on LinkedIn

LinkedIn is the primary channel at investor stage. The audience is: investors, sustainability-focused procurement leads, water utilities, DTU/research networks, policy-adjacent readers.

**Tone on LinkedIn:** Professional but not corporate. Precise but not technical. Confident but never arrogant.

### Post types that fit the brand

**1. Problem framing posts**
State the industrial problem in one short paragraph. End with Vasuqi's position — not a product pitch, just a clear claim about where the solution sits.

> Water treatment infrastructure was built for a different era of regulation. The EU Water Framework Directive has new targets. The existing toolkit doesn't reach them.  
>
> That's the gap we're in.

**2. Technology clarity posts**
Explain one thing — just one — about how photocatalytic disinfection works. Not a lecture. One idea, plainly stated.

> Visible light activates a surface reaction that breaks down pathogens. No heat. No chemicals. No moving parts. The reactor is as simple as the idea.

**3. Milestone posts**
DTU affiliation, BII backing, research progress, pilot conversations. State the fact first. Add one sentence of context.

> Vasuqi received backing from Business Innovation Invest Denmark. We're building toward our first pilot in municipal water treatment.

**4. Regulatory / market context posts**
News about water regulation, EU directives, industrial water quality — react briefly and place Vasuqi in the frame without over-claiming.

> The EU's revised Urban Wastewater Treatment Directive sets 2030 targets that existing secondary treatment cannot meet at scale. This is the market condition Vasuqi is built for.

**5. Founder/team voice posts (from CEO Adarsh)**
Personal-ish but never casual. The founder can write in first person — this humanises without breaking brand tone.

> I spent three years on this problem because the science was simple and the solution wasn't built yet. It still isn't — but we're building it.

## What not to post

- **No buzzword stacking.** Not: "Leveraging cutting-edge sustainable deep-tech solutions to revolutionize water purification ecosystems."
- **No urgency theatre.** Not: "The water crisis is HERE and it's CRITICAL that we act NOW."
- **No competitor comparison.** Not: "Unlike UV or ozonation, Vasuqi..."
- **No over-qualifying.** Not: "We believe we may potentially be positioned to address..."
- **No emoji-heavy posts.** One emoji maximum, if any. None is also correct.
- **No vague sustainability signalling.** Not: "We care about our planet." Instead: name the mechanism, name the regulation, name the number.

## Format rules for social posts

| Element | Rule |
|---|---|
| Opening line | Short. Often a single sentence or fragment. Never a question. |
| Length | LinkedIn: 3–6 short paragraphs max. Under 250 words. |
| Hashtags | 2–4 max. Always at the end. Not mid-post. |
| Tone check | Read it back. Does it sound like something a confident engineer would say? Good. Does it sound like a press release? Rewrite. |
| Images | If using one: cool tones, clean composition, no warm colours. Product renders, water imagery, abstract light. No stock-photo-of-smiling-team. |

## Tone on other platforms

**Instagram** (if used): Visual-first. Short captions — 1–3 sentences max. Let the image carry the claim. Ambient water imagery, light effects, blueprint-style diagrams. No product comparisons. Brand-voice rules apply identically.

**X / Twitter** (if used): Single sharp claim per post. The anti-hype rule is most important here — the platform rewards the punchy negative-space statement. "No chemicals. No sludge. Just light." performs better than anything that explains.

---

## The single test

Before posting anything, ask: **Does this sound like Vasuqi, or does it sound like every other cleantech startup?**

If the answer is "every other startup" — shorten it, remove the hedging, and lead with the claim.
