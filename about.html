About me

I'm Edda, an Icelandic digital designer with a BA in Anthropology, a Master's in Tourism, and a degree in Multimedia Design from Zealand Academy. I came to design through a curiosity about people — and stayed for the craft of making complex things usable. I focus on UX research, visual design, front-end development, and project management.

How I work

I start with people, not pixels. Before I design anything, I do the UX research — interviews, usability tests, data analysis. My anthropology training means I dig into why people behave the way they do, not just what they click on. Depending on the project, I work within Double Diamond, Design Thinking, or Lean Startup — moving from research through information architecture and wireframes to tested prototypes, keeping the team aligned with clear structure and open communication.

What I can do

I research, design, and code. I bring an anthropological lens to user research — then design responsive interfaces in Figma and build the front-end in HTML, SCSS, and JavaScript, often using AI tools to accelerate both design and code. I also create illustrations and motion graphics. I've led project coordination for teams of up to nine people using agile methods.
